Theoretical Foundation

∬

Double Integration

A double integral $\displaystyle \iint_R f(x,y)\,dA$ extends single integration to functions over a two-dimensional region. It accumulates quantities across an area by integrating over both $x$ and $y$ variables.

⊞

Area of a 2D Region

The area of a bounded planar region can be computed using a double integral. For a vertical region between two curves:

$$ A = \iint_R 1\,dA $$ $$ A = \int_a^b \int_{g(x)}^{f(x)} dy\,dx $$
⇆

Iterated Integrals

A double integral is evaluated as an iterated integral by integrating one variable at a time. The inner integral is evaluated first, followed by the outer integral.

$$ \int_a^b \int_{g(x)}^{f(x)} f(x,y)\,dy\,dx $$
⨯

Regions & Limits of Integration

The limits describe the boundaries of the region $R$. For a vertically simple region: $g(x) \le y \le f(x)$, $a \le x \le b$. Choosing correct limits is essential for evaluating double integrals.

The General Double Integral Formula
$\displaystyle A \;=\; \iint_R 1\,dA \;=\; \int_{a}^{b} \int_{g(x)}^{f(x)} dy\,dx$

where $y$ varies from the lower curve $g(x)$ to the upper curve $f(x)$, and $x$ varies from $a$ to $b$.
